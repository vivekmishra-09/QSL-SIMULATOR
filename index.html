What is Quantum Signal Loss?

Quantum Signal Loss (QSL) is a new theoretical framework to represent how fragile quantum information deteriorates over vast cosmic distances — especially in environments where classical metrics like signal-to-noise ratio and bit error rate fail to capture the real picture.

Traditional communication models only consider visible disruptions. But in **quantum-scale communication**, even undetectable interference, spacetime fluctuations, or decoherence fields can collapse information integrity. QSL attempts to quantify this invisible decay.

Use Case: Deep Space Signal

Imagine a quantum probe sending information back from the edge of the solar system. The signal may technically reach Earth, but the quantum coherence — the core identity of the signal — is already breaking down due to cumulative cosmic interference.

QSL acts as a scalar indicator between 0 and 1, showing how "alive" the quantum signal still is — not based on hardware, but based on the fabric of spacetime and interaction history.
